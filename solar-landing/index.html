Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before going solar.

A 3 kW residential system typically costs ₹1.5–2 lakh before subsidies. After the central government subsidy (up to ₹78,000), net cost can be as low as ₹72,000. Our team will provide a precise quote after a free site assessment.
Most residential installations are completed in 1–2 days. The entire process from signing to system activation (including permits and DISCOM approvals) typically takes 2–4 weeks.
Yes! Modern solar panels generate electricity from diffused light, not just direct sunlight. On cloudy days they produce 10–25% of peak capacity. With grid tie-in and net metering, your lights will always stay on.
With government subsidies included, most homes achieve payback in 3–5 years. After that, you essentially get free electricity for 20+ more years, saving several lakhs over the panel lifetime.
For grid-connected systems, batteries are optional. Net metering means the grid itself acts as your "battery". If you live in an area with frequent power cuts, we offer hybrid systems with lithium battery storage.
Any residential consumer in India with a valid electricity connection is eligible. The subsidy applies to on-grid rooftop solar systems up to 10 kW. Commercial and industrial consumers have separate incentives.
